OUR AUTHENTICITY

Traditional cuisine is passed down from one generation to the next. It also operates as an expression of cultural identity. Immigrants bring the food of their countries with them wherever they go and cooking traditional food is a way of preserving their culture when they move to new places.

Pakistani cuisine has Indian roots (found in the form of the usage of heavy spices), Irani influences, Afghani, Persian, and Western influences. Since the Mugal Empire ruled around 1526, Pakistan adopted part of their cuisine that included the herbs and spices, almonds, and the raisins in their dishes.
